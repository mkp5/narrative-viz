Definition: This is a comparison of life expectancy of each country against the world population. This helps to observe the change in trend of this property against the rest of the world.

Years of healthy life can be increased 5-10 years Worldwide, healthy life expectancy can be increased by 5-10 years if governments and individuals make combined efforts against the major health risks in each region, the World Health Organization (WHO) says in its new yearly report.
As per WHO' World Health Report 2002, reducing the below risks can lower the mortality rate and boost the life expectancy of mankind.

  • Underweight/under-nutrition
  • Unsafe sex
  • High blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Tobacco Use
  • Unsafe Water and Sanitation
  • Iron deficiency
